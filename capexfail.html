Jesus Mancilla-Garcia 04/09/2026

I was walking in downtown Palo Alto and chanced upon a museum of Cryptopunks. The display was a glimpse into the future of art: digitally displayed, high-res color communication. But has the merit of an art piece ever been who owns it? We're in a world where Andy Warhol could've sold every frame of Marilyn Diptych for 100's of $1,000's. This capitalism first-look happens in Palo Alto, as per usual.

Meta's recent layoff purge is absolutely AI-washing. Meta's pivot to AI has been lackluster. Models with names like Llama or Muse Spark (which could've been just Muse) promise high performance but don't deliver, especially compared to competitors. They can't possibly be offloading core operations with internal AI tools. Meta is trying to cover for the metaverse CapEx failure by using its employees as scapegoats.

That CapEx is wicked, tho. Consequently, stakeholders forgiving Meta is a vermilion flag. $100,000+ on a "non-fungible token" art piece, while the supporting infra remains hollow, is outrageous. This century has a certain tolerance, or ignorance, for burning historic amounts of money.

All it takes to forgive Meta is the U.S. Commander in Chief making the market swing in favor of the top 1% with a "positive" comment on America's war in Iran, Meta stock goes up some points and thus many billions in value, and we're all reminded Meta has billions to burn. Billions. And in those billions retail investors can make thousands. What a lopsided and losing equation for retail investors.

Some recent numbers: Google can spend $700 million on a lawsuit settlement alone. Amazon has a $200 billion spending plan for 2026. Oracle lays off thousands, after throwing a $84 million conference, then hires a single employee with a $20M+ package.

The goal of modern ventures is to become this inflated enterprise that transforms billions in funding to billions in regrettable CapEx in the name of growth, just like their established predecessors. This is modern day VC. This system is unsustainable. It's wasteful, the equivalent of a trash island the size of Texas in our oceans.

This isn't the same market that allowed previous generations to attain wealth.

The signal of tech-enabled wealth inequality is bearish for now. Lots of wealth is being made here in Silicon Valley because of AI, but the bubble will pop. Dummy CapEx or layoffs in the name of AI point to a market correction that must happen before the true jump to AI wealth happens, and it's looking like it won't be democratic when we get there.
